Mental Health Tips For Students. See for yourself.

✅ 1. Maintain a Balanced Routine Set a daily schedule including study, meals, sleep, and relaxation. Avoid overloading yourself—plan short, focused study sessions.

✅ 2. Prioritize Sleep Aim for 7–9 hours of sleep. Lack of sleep can cause irritability, anxiety, and poor focus.

✅ 3. Eat a Nutritious Diet Include fruits, vegetables, whole grains, and protein-rich foods. Stay hydrated—avoid excessive junk food and caffeine.

✅ 4. Exercise Regularly Physical activity boosts mood and reduces stress hormones. Even 20–30 minutes of walking, yoga, or cycling helps.

✅ 5. Take Breaks During Study Follow the Pomodoro Technique (25 mins study, 5 mins break). Breaks refresh the mind and prevent burnout.

✅ 6. Talk About Your Feelings Share with friends, family, or a counselor. Bottling up emotions increases anxiety.

✅ 7. Limit Social Media Use Excessive scrolling can lead to comparison and anxiety. Use apps mindfully and take digital detoxes when needed.

✅ 8. Practice Mindfulness or Meditation Helps with focus, calmness, and emotional control. Just 10 minutes a day can make a big difference.

✅ 9. Set Realistic Goals Break large tasks into small, achievable steps. Celebrate small wins to stay motivated.

✅ 10. Seek Help When Needed Don't hesitate to approach a school counselor or mental health professional. Asking for help is a strength, not a weakness.
