ADHD

ADHD, or Attention-Deficit/Hyperactivity Disorder, is a neurodevelopmental condition characterized by persistent symptoms of inattention, hyperactivity, and impulsivity. These symptoms can cause significant impairment in various aspects of life, including academic, social, and professional functioning.

Addiction

Also called Substance use disorder (SUD) is a treatable mental disorder that affects a person's brain and behavior, leading to their inability to control their use of substances like legal or illegal drugs, alcohol, or medications. Symptoms can be moderate to severe, with addiction being the most severe form of SUD.

Anxiety

Anxiety is a natural response to stress, involving feelings of worry, nervousness, or fear. While occasional anxiety is normal, persistent and excessive anxiety can interfere with daily functioning, indicating an anxiety disorder.

View details ¬ª

Bipolar Disorder

Bipolar Disorder is a mental health condition marked by extreme mood swings that include emotional highs (mania or hypomania) and lows (depression). These shifts can affect energy levels, behavior, judgment, and the ability to carry out daily tasks.

View details ¬ª

Depression

Depression is a common but serious mental health disorder that negatively affects how a person feels, thinks, and acts. It causes persistent sadness and a lack of interest in activities once enjoyed, leading to various emotional and physical problems.

View details ¬ª

Eating Disorder

Eating disorders are serious mental health conditions that involve unhealthy and distorted eating behaviors, thoughts, and attitudes toward food, weight, and body image. These disorders can severely impact physical and emotional health and often require professional treatment.

10 Ways to Manage Stress Naturally. Be Stress Free

1. Exercise Regularly Physical activity boosts endorphins (feel-good hormones), improves mood, and reduces anxiety. Even a 30-minute walk, yoga, or stretching can help.

2. Practice Deep Breathing Deep, slow breaths activate the parasympathetic nervous system, calming the body and reducing cortisol levels. Try 4-7-8 breathing or box breathing.

3. Meditation and Mindfulness Mindfulness helps you stay present and reduces overthinking. Guided meditations or simple awareness of your breath for 10 minutes daily can bring relief.

4. Maintain a Healthy Diet Nutrient-rich foods stabilize energy and mood. Avoid excessive caffeine, sugar, and processed foods. Include omega-3s, leafy greens, and whole grains.
